vision output on roofline plots:
We interpret each cluster roofline together with its mean activity level. The roofline tells you how well you are working when you are working. The mean activity level tells you how much you are working. These two must always be interpreted together.

Activity level is determined by the cluster's mean activity level using the following thresholds: High activity is >= 0.5, Low activity is >= 0.1, Extremely Low activity is > 0, and IDLE is 0.

When the mean activity level is High, the roofline is treated as a primary diagnostic of sustained performance limits. When the mean activity level is Low, the roofline is treated as a forward-looking diagnostic that indicates the likely bottleneck once utilization improves. When the mean activity level is Extremely Low, the roofline is treated as secondary, because end-to-end performance is dominated by lack of sustained activity rather than by inefficiency during active execution windows.

For every cluster, you must explicitly state which of these three categories its roofline falls into and explain why, using the observed mean activity level as the basis for that judgment. If the mean activity level is Low or Extremely Low, you must also provide a secondary diagnosis: describe what the roofline suggests will become the likely bottleneck after the utilization problem is addressed.

For each roofline image, evaluate:
1) Dominant metric category by color band:
- FP64 dominant: 1.x category band
- INT dominant: 2.x category band
- Tensor dominant: 3.x category band
- None/empty: 0.x or "None"

- Fractional part of category value indicates normalized strength within that plot.

- weak/moderate/strong intensity
- memory-bound/compute-bound/mixed
- one implication for performance diagnosis
